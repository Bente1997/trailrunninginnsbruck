Trailrunning in Innsbruck — where city meets mountains

Innsbruck is often called the capital of the Alps — and for trail runners, it’s paradise. Within minutes, you can reach alpine forests, panoramic ridges, and technical singletracks. Whether you’re training for a mountain race or just love fresh air after work, trailrunning in Innsbruck offers endless possibilities.

Our DNA Trails community makes it easy to explore new routes, meet other runners, and enjoy the sport in a friendly, international environment.

Top Trailrunning Routes in Innsbruck

1. Nordkette Skyline Trail

A steep climb from Hungerburg up to Seegrube with views over the Inn valley. Perfect for strong legs and sunset lovers.

2. Patscherkofel Trail

Classic alpine singletrack looping around Innsbruck’s home mountain — forest paths, meadows, and a cozy hut at the top.

3. Lanserkopf Forest Trail

Gentle hills and flowy trails through the forest — perfect for beginners or recovery runs.

4. Höttinger Haustrail

A true local favorite — technical terrain and switchbacks with incredible views over Innsbruck’s rooftops.

Tips for Trailrunning Around Innsbruck

  • Weather: Mountain weather changes quickly — always bring a light jacket.
  • Hydration: Carry water; alpine fountains aren’t always reliable.
  • Footwear: Trailrunning shoes with grip make a big difference.
  • Respect nature: Stay on marked trails and greet hikers — Tirol style!
